Tourist ATTRACTIONS

Tourist spots in Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is a great Brazilian city by the sea, famous for the beaches of Copacabana and Ipanema, an attraction is the top of Corcovado, and for the Sugarloaf Mountain, a granite peak with cable cars to its summit. The exciting Carnival, with floats, extravagant costumes and samba dancers, is considered the largest in the world.
01 / 05
Cristo Redentor is an art deco statue depicting Jesus Christ, located at the top of Corcovado Hill, 709 meters above sea level, in Tijuca National Park, overlooking most of the city of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
In 2007 he was informally elected as one of the seven wonders of the modern world.
02 / 05
Natural Monument of Morro do Pão de Açúcar and Urca is a hill complex located in the Urca neighborhood, in the city of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It consists of the Morro do Pão de Açúcar and the Morro da Urca.
03 / 05
Stadium Jornalista Mário Filho, better known as Maracanã, or affectionately as Maraca, is a football stadium located in Rio de Janeiro. It has an incredible 24 meters high and a capacity of up to 78,838 people.
04 / 05
Arpoador is a beach and rock formation, both located in the neighborhood of Ipanema, in the state of Rio de Janeiro, in Brazil. It is located between the beaches of Diabo and Ipanema. It presents restricted space, as it is a small beach, about 500 meters long.
05 / 05
The Museum of Contemporary Art of Niterói (MAC) is a museum of contemporary Brazilian art located in the city of Niterói, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The work was inaugurated on September 2, 1996. Designed by architect Oscar Niemeyer, MAC became one of the postcards of Niterói.
